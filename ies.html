Best Paper · IES 2024 ▼

Compact and Lightweight Foldable Umbrella Heatshield for CanSat

8 pages · 15 figures · 3 tables · 18 refs
Why It Won Best Paper
  • Multi-function mechanism (heatshield does 3 jobs simultaneously) — signals engineering efficiency
  • Actual test flight data from rocket launch — proves the concept at real scale
  • Material selection through comparative analysis across 6 candidates, not gut feeling
  • Acknowledges parachute failure + explains root cause (late inflation from blocked airflow)
Figure Breakdown
Mission overview diagram CAD renders (exploded views) Leadscrew mechanism drawing 3D assembly renders 3-step deployment photos Flight software flowchart Altitude vs packet count graph Tilt stability scatter plot Actual launch photo
Results Method

Comparison tables (density, durability, melt temp, cost) + line graphs of sensor telemetry + MAE stability metrics. Calculated vs. actual comparison.

Best Paper · IES 2025 ▼

Thermal Deformation Analysis of CanSat Frame Materials using FEM

6 pages · 12 figures · 6 tables · 11 refs
Why It Won Best Paper
  • Fills an explicitly named research gap ("comparative FEM at assembled system level — not studied before")
  • FEM heat maps = visual proof more impactful than any table (color-coded stress distribution)
  • Makes a real engineering trade-off recommendation (polycarbonate vs. composite), not just reporting data
  • Rigorous FEM with mesh sensitivity analysis documented
  • Future work explicitly planned: thermal chamber testing to confirm simulation
Figure Breakdown
Chemical structure diagrams CanSat cross-section with zones FEM mesh visualization Boundary condition diagram Deformation heat maps ×3 Von-Mises stress maps ×3 Technical drawings with dimensions
Results Method

FEM color visualization (false-color scale, red=high/blue=low) + comparative table (3 materials × 2 metrics). Clear winner per criterion, not single winner overall.

  • Each gamification element tied to learning psychology literature

Full 7×10 respondent response matrix (1 / 0.5 / 0 scale) + step-by-step formula calculation + threshold comparison. Transparent, nothing cherry-picked.

IES-Specific Patterns

Observations specific to IES across all analyzed papers.

✓ DO
6–8 pages

All 4 papers are 6–8 pages. 5 pages may feel light; 9+ may get cut.

9–13 figures

Visual density signals completeness. Papers with <6 figures tend to feel unfinished.

System + Flowchart + Results chart

Three figure types appear in every paper. Missing one is a common rejection risk.

Name your research gap explicitly

Best Paper states it as its own sentence. Others imply it — but explicit wins.

Define success criteria before results

e.g., "Target descent rate ≤ 20 m/s" stated in methodology, then confirmed in results.

Acknowledge partial failures

The heatshield best paper openly reports a failure and explains the mechanism. Reviewers trust this more than "everything worked perfectly."

✗ DON'T
Only tables, no charts

Zero accepted papers use tables as their sole result presentation. Add at least one chart.

Vague "future work"

"Further research is needed" does not count. Name the specific next step.

Justify choices by preference

"We selected X because it is popular" — always replace with a reference or measured comparison.

Design without validation

Minimum acceptable: simulation results. All 4 accepted papers have empirical or user-tested evidence.

No comparison to alternatives

Every accepted paper compares at least 2 options. Reviewers expect to see alternatives rejected.

Key insight: Figures are never decorative. Each one has a specific analytical job — validate design, compare alternatives, show results, or prove implementation. Papers with only tables and no charts are consistently weaker.

Figure Combinations That Work

Minimum viable figure set for IES based on all accepted papers.

Required
🏗

System Architecture

Block diagram or component diagram showing all major parts and their connections. Every single accepted paper has this.

Required
🔄

Process Flow / Flowchart

How data or control moves through the system. Decision diamonds, process boxes, arrows. Shows reviewer you understand the full pipeline.

Required
📊

Results Chart

At least one chart or graph visualizing your results data. Bar chart, line graph, scatter plot, heat map — any format. Tables alone are not enough.

Required
⚖️

Comparative Visual

Side-by-side comparison of 2+ options, materials, or methods. Table or paired figures. Shows design choices are data-driven.

Strong
📷

Implementation Photo / Screenshot

Hardware built and photographed, or screenshots of a running system. 3 of 4 accepted papers include this. FEM heat maps serve the same "proof of work" function.

Strong
📐

Technical Drawing with Dimensions

Annotated diagram with measurements, labels, or specifications. Signals engineering rigor. Present in winning CanSat and Best Paper.
